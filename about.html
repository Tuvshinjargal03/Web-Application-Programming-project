Discover the key pollutants we monitor and their potential impacts:

  • PM2.5: Fine particles that penetrate deep into the lungs, leading to respiratory and cardiovascular issues.
  • PM10: Larger particles that can cause breathing problems and worsen asthma.
  • CO2: A significant greenhouse gas contributing to climate change.
  • SO2: Emitted from industrial processes, leading to acid rain and respiratory problems.
  • NOx: Gases contributing to smog and acid rain.
  • NH3: Ammonia from agriculture affecting particulate matter formation.

Learn practical ways to minimize your exposure to air pollution and stay safe:

1. Monitor Air Quality Levels

Keep track of air quality in your area using reliable tools:

  • AirNow ‚Äì Real-time air quality updates in the U.S.
  • World Air Quality Index ‚Äì Global air quality data and forecasts.

Check the Air Quality Index (AQI):

  • 0-50 (Good): Safe for outdoor activities.
  • 51-100 (Moderate): Sensitive individuals should limit exposure.
  • 101+ (Unhealthy): Everyone should avoid prolonged outdoor exposure.

2. Limit Outdoor Exposure

  • Avoid outdoor activities during peak pollution hours (morning and evening rush hours).
  • Stay indoors when the AQI is unhealthy or during pollution events like wildfires or dust storms.
  • Use air purifiers at home to improve indoor air quality.

3. Protect Yourself When Outdoors

  • Wear a Mask: Use high-quality masks (e.g., N95, KN95) to filter fine particles (PM2.5).
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water to help your body flush out pollutants.
  • Protect Your Eyes: Wear sunglasses to prevent irritation from airborne particles.

4. Adopt a Healthy Lifestyle

  • Eat antioxidant-rich foods (e.g., fruits, vegetables) to combat pollution's effects on your body.
  • Exercise indoors to maintain health without exposing yourself to outdoor pollutants.
