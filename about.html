Project Overview

The Weather and Air Quality Dashboard is designed to provide users with comprehensive real-time and historical weather and air quality data. It is an essential tool for individuals, researchers, and policymakers aiming to track environmental metrics, understand trends, and take informed decisions.

Air Quality Metrics

Our dashboard monitors a range of air quality components that are critical for assessing environmental health:

  • PM2.5 (Particulate Matter): Fine particles that can penetrate deep into the lungs and cause respiratory issues.
  • PM10 (Particulate Matter): Larger particles that affect breathing and exacerbate conditions like asthma.
  • CO2 (Carbon Dioxide): A key greenhouse gas influencing climate change and air quality.
  • SO2 (Sulfur Dioxide): A pollutant often resulting from industrial processes, contributing to acid rain.
  • NOx (Nitrogen Oxides): Gases that play a major role in forming smog and acid rain.
  • NH3 (Ammonia): A byproduct of agriculture that contributes to the formation of particulate matter.
  • VOC (Volatile Organic Compounds): Organic chemicals that evaporate into the air, affecting indoor and outdoor air quality.

By analyzing these metrics, our dashboard provides insights into pollution levels and their potential impact on health and the environment.

Weather Tracking

Our platform integrates real-time weather tracking features, providing:

  • Temperature: View current and historical temperature trends to plan outdoor activities.
  • Humidity: Monitor moisture levels in the air, essential for comfort and health.
  • Wind Speed: Analyze wind conditions, critical for air quality dispersion and weather forecasting.
  • Visibility: Assess atmospheric clarity, vital for transportation safety.
  • Sunrise & Sunset Times: Precise daily information for lifestyle and photography enthusiasts.

Dashboard Features

Here are the key functionalities offered by the dashboard:

  • Interactive line, bar, and doughnut charts to visualize trends and distributions.
  • Real-time updates using APIs for accurate and timely data.
  • Responsive design to ensure usability across devices, including desktops, tablets, and smartphones.
  • Color-coded indicators to quickly assess air quality levels (e.g., green for good, red for hazardous).

Technologies Used

  • HTML5: For structuring the content on the web pages.
  • CSS3: For styling and layout, ensuring a responsive and visually appealing design.
  • JavaScript: For dynamic updates, user interactions, and chart rendering.
  • Chart.js: To create interactive and visually engaging charts.
  • OpenWeather API: For fetching real-time weather and air quality data.

Why This Dashboard Matters

This dashboard helps users:

  • Understand the impact of pollution on daily life and health.
  • Make informed decisions about outdoor activities based on weather and air quality.
  • Contribute to awareness campaigns with accurate data insights.
  • Analyze trends for climate and pollution studies.
